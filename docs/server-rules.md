# :police_officer: Server Rules

Level Up SMP is a casual, community-driven server built on trust, respect, and shared enjoyment of the world. Most of us know each other, but these rules exist to make sure everyone -- especially newcomers -- understands what kind of place this is.

This is not just a Minecraft server. It is a shared world. Act accordingly.

---

## 1. Respect the world and the work within it

Every build, farm, path, shop, and project represents someone’s time and creativity. Treat them with care.

## 2. Do not grief, steal, or sabotage

Do not destroy, alter, take from, or interfere with anything that does not belong to you unless you have clear permission.

## 3. Ask before touching another player’s space

If it involves someone else’s base, storage, animals, redstone, farms, or plans, ask first instead of assuming.

## 4. Give others room to build and grow

Do not crowd other players, build too close without approval, or expand into areas that are clearly part of someone else’s space.

## 5. Treat shared spaces like they matter

Community areas, roads, portals, farms, and public resources should be maintained and used respectfully.

## 6. Keep PvP, pranks, and chaos consensual

Friendly chaos can be fun. Unwanted chaos is just being a problem. Make sure everyone involved is on board first.

## 7. Do not cheat or abuse exploits

No hacked clients, x-ray, dupes, or abuse of unintended mechanics that give unfair advantages or undermine trust.

## 8. Build responsibly and avoid causing lag

Do not create lag-heavy farms, redstone, mob buildup, or other systems that negatively affect the server for everyone else.

## 9. Communicate clearly and handle issues like an adult

If something goes wrong, talk about it honestly. Ask questions, own mistakes, and try to resolve problems without drama.

## 10. Remember this is a shared world, not a single-player one

Play in a way that makes the server better for other people, not just more convenient for yourself.

---

## In Short

Be the kind of player people are happy to share a world with.

If you are ever unsure whether something is okay, ask first.
